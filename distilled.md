# In Tech We Trust — Distilled Reference

## Meta

- **Authors**: Rufus Pollock, Rosie Bell, Sylvie Barbier (Life Itself / Univ. Cambridge)
- **Status**: White paper v1.0-beta, Feb 2026
- **Published**: https://secondrenaissance.net/papers/tech
- **Tone**: Essayistic, philosophical, urgent but measured. Draws on Buddhism (Thich Nhat Hanh), critical theory (Arendt, Zuboff), evolutionary thought. Not anti-tech per se — critiques uncritical *deference* to tech. Activist-intellectual voice; calls for paradigm shift, not policy tweaks.
- **Series context**: Part of Life Itself's "Second Renaissance" series examining civilizational crisis and cultural paradigm shift. A foundational paper covers the broader paradigm shift idea; this one addresses technology specifically as a first case because tech has become not just a tool but a primary organizing logic of modernity.

---

## Abstract / Core Thesis

Modern humanity doesn't just *use* technology — we perceive the world in its image and invest it with sacred authority. Tech has shifted from servant to master, becoming a cultural ideology. The acceleration of AI brings this to crisis point. Two deep shifts are needed:

1. **From** out-of-control tech (driven by collective action problems rooted in individualism) **→ To** collective restraint via a worldview of *interbeing*
2. **From** tyranny of tech (sustained by rationalist illusion of free agency) **→ To** wiser choice grounded in holistic human nature and inner capacities

---

## Key Features of the Modern Paradigm (as they relate to tech)

Six interlocking views/assumptions the essay identifies as constituting the modern worldview:

1. **Material reality as paramount** — Only the measurable is real; subjective/spiritual dismissed
2. **Myth of progress** — History as inevitable improvement; more tech = always better
3. **Technology as primary cosmological metaphor** — Life/cosmos viewed as machine; mechanistic worldview supplants relational/sacred views
4. **Individualism** — Separate competing entities; autonomy and self-interest as guiding values
5. **Rationalism** — Reason as ultimate knowledge source and defining human quality; mind-as-computer metaphor reinforces this
6. **Homo economicus** — Rational self-interested agents making free choices in competitive markets

## Section 1: Co-evolving with Tools

- Humans have *always* co-evolved with tech (fire → digestion/social habits; clothing → bodies; tools → adaptation). We've been "cyborgs" since prehistory.
- Resistance to new tech is ancient and often looks futile in hindsight.
- **What's different now**: speed, scale, complexity, and *irreversibility* of tech's influence. Growing wisdom gap — tech's impact escalates while our capacity to choose well is increasingly compromised.

## Section 2: Late-Modern Relationship with Technology

### 2.1 Technology as God

Tech has replaced religion as source of awe, trust, authority, and salvation:

- **Divine mystery**: Tech is so advanced it's indistinguishable from magic; we trust without understanding
- **"In tech we trust"**: We submit to algorithmic capitalism, smartphone addiction, attention-hacking without question. Even climate policy relies on blind faith in future tech breakthroughs (carbon removal)
- **Salvation**: Cryopreservation, transhumanism — tech as path to eternal life
- **Divine right of technologists**: Tech founders (Jobs, Musk) as cultural high priests; their opinions treated as relevant to everything

### 2.2 From Servant to Master

Tech was a tremendous servant (health, comfort, communication). But "innovation" now means technological innovation only, sidelining human dimensions. Restricting tech is taboo/naive. Tech has become a dominant ideology without the human dimensions essential to any political programme.

**Utility and the rational fallacy**: Tech adoption is legitimized by a narrative of utility (need arises → innovation meets it). Rationalism/homo economicus underpins this: sovereign consumers freely choosing what's best. But:
- Behavioral economics, evolutionary psychology show decision-making is emotional, heuristic, impulsive, belief-driven *before* rational
- "Externality" is fiction — climate crisis proves there's no separate world to export harm into
- Smartphone addiction debunks homo economicus for "normal" adults

**Case study — Zen Master at Google (2013)**: Thich Nhat Hanh suggested a mindfulness bell feature for phones. Engineer objected: "wouldn't we be imposing?" TNH: people can't distinguish real needs from false needs (eating to escape suffering vs. hunger). The engineer's response perfectly demonstrates naive faith in rational free choice. Key insight: *choice is useless absent the capacity to choose well*.

**Tech as creator**: Tech doesn't just direct us — it reshapes cognition, attention, bodies (Carr's *The Shallows*: brains rewired for short attention/superficial engagement). What's alarming now: unprecedented pace of adoption, complexity of interaction, depth of impact on minds/bodies, and the growing **wisdom gap**.

### 2.3 AI: Logical Conclusion

AI amplifies all prior patterns at warp speed. Dario Amodei quote: "deeply unclear whether our systems possess the maturity to wield" the coming power.

- ChatGPT (late 2022) brought AI risks into mainstream debate
- Direct harms already visible: education disruption, creative industry displacement, water/energy costs
- AGI: whether likely or not, the scale of uncertainty demands precautionary scrutiny
- Most realistic AI threat: not consciousness but supreme power *without* embodied consciousness — accelerating our inability to resist tech's authority
- **No experts exist** for the social impact of emerging tech. By the time relevant expertise develops, the industry is powerful enough to resist dissent (cf. Big Oil vs climate science)

## Section 3: Toward a Wiser Relationship

### 3.1 Beyond Collective Action Problems

The AI race is a classic **collective action problem** (CAP): all would benefit from cooperation, but short-term competition drives everyone toward long-term harm. "If we don't, others will."

- All major global threats (climate, nuclear, AI) rest on CAPs
- Unilateral ethical action doesn't help — power asymmetry favors competitors
- Schmachtenberger: the thing that extracts more, grows more, increases violence capability *wins* — "plus exponential tech, at planetary boundaries, self-terminates"

**Root cause**: Modern paradigm of separateness/competition. Neo-Darwinian story of separate entities competing, amplified by modern institutions (markets, education as contests, consumerism). Zero-sum logic entrenched.

**Antidote — shared meaning**: Humanity *has* overcome CAPs historically, often through emotional/spiritual force — deep belonging and shared duty. Example: **Bali's Subak irrigation** (9th century) — cooperative water management integrated with water temples, sacred culture, TRI HITA KARANA philosophy uniting spirit/human/nature. Collectivism and metaphysical belief co-arose.

### Interbeing

Core proposed worldview shift. Term from Thich Nhat Hanh: nothing exists in isolation; to exist is to be fundamentally interconnected. Key arguments:

- Interdependency is fact, not sentiment — nobody wins a competition ending in shared destruction
- Separateness as illusion is why acting against commons ultimately undermines the whole
- Not alien to modern life: families demonstrate it daily (loved ones we'd die for); historically we've widened circles of care
- Not denial of individual — recognizing separateness as partial view, restoring balance
- Net direction of human history: widening circles of concern (taxes for collective wellbeing, etc.)
- Cultivation accessible even secularly through imagination (tracing dependencies of daily life)

### 3.2 Escaping Tech Tyranny: Better Choices

**Paradigm shifts emerge in "pockets"** — small groups of innovators doing conscious culture-making, acting as cultural leadership ("islands of coherence"). Imagines a "Y-Combinator for the Second Renaissance."

**Two levels of wiser choice-making**:

1. **Everyday spaciousness**: Pausing before reacting; refining embodied signals that assist reasoning. Prioritizing slowness, sensing, discernment over instant gratification. Requires a tech economy that doesn't incentivize attention-hacking.

2. **Deeper wisdom**: For far-reaching societal choices. Combines reasoning/experience/complex thinking with *trans-rational* qualities: intuition, insight, sensitivity, collective care. Modern tech-driven culture has sidelined cultivation of these. A wise society wouldn't abandon reason but restore wisdom as a pillar of sense-making alongside it.

---

## Conclusion (compressed)

Tech's godlike status is a vicious circle: the more machines empower us, the more we defer; reducing ourselves to mechanistic logic, becoming less capable of resisting. Restoring wise relationship requires transforming our impoverished computational view of humanness. Two moves: (1) a worldview of interbeing to escape collective action problems, and (2) restoration of neglected inner capacities (intuitive, embodied, trans-rational knowing) for wiser choice. Technology must return to being servant of what is most human, relational, and conducive to life.
